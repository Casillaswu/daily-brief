▲Executive Briefing

What's going on this week

Three things in PH iGaming this week. PAGCOR dropped a 1.5% rebate cap on May 7 — every brand's pricing has to be rebuilt. PlayTime's group rebrand is complete: Miss Universe Philippines as a long-term partner, officially out of the iGaming-only frame. DigiPlus Q1 net income is down 33%; e-wallet delinking + fuel inflation are cooling the whole market.

1. PAGCOR May 7 memo: rebates and cashback capped at 1.5% of turnover.
New rule: slots / e-Bingo / numeric games / sports all capped at 1.5% of player turnover (gross bets / deposit), or 15% of net losses as an alternative. Programs can't be stacked. That directly oversizes CasinoPlus 2.6% cashback, PlayTime 2.55% unlimited cashback, and Bet88 5% weekly MLB — all three have to rebuild their promo structures. The memo is explicitly framed as "leveling the playing field" — aimed at the heavily capitalized operators that have been using oversized rebates to acquire and retain players. (Source: IAG 5/10 · iGB)

2. PlayTime's group expansion is now official, and it's no longer just an iGaming brand.
playtime.ph went from a placeholder/redirect to a full group page this week: PT Idols (creator economy) + PT Sports (sports/esports) + PT Gaming (casino, now at ptgaming.ph) — three verticals side by side. The site lists long-term partnerships: Miss Universe Philippines 2026, Mister Pilipinas Worldwide, Metro Manila Film Festival 2025, Manila Times Golf Cup 2026, Panagbenga Festival 2026. Bilyonaryo reported on May 7 ("PlayTime Crowns its Queens") covering both the May 2 MUPH crowning (Bea Millan-Windorski of La Union) and the new "Miss PlayTime Universe" title, awarded on May 10 at the Miss Philippines coronation ball. This is the first PH iGaming operator to systematically break out of the "gambling brand" frame via entertainment/pageantry/media IP. (Source: playtime.ph site recon 5/11 · Bilyonaryo 5/7)

3. DigiPlus Q1: net income -33%, revenue -25%, EBITDA -42%. The market is cooling.
Q1 26 results (May 5–6): net income P2.8B (-33% YoY), revenue P17.2B vs P23.0B (-25%), EBITDA P2.6B (-42%). Blamed on e-wallet delinking + fuel inflation suppressing spending. Sequentially it's stabilizing (revenue flat from Q4 25 P17.3B; net income +15% QoQ). The release also recapped the ArenaPlus × NBA "first official betting partner in the Philippines" deal — but that's not W20 news. The Mall of Asia launch event ran on April 12, the official GlobeNewswire PR went out April 27, and Yogonet picked it up April 30. PBA + PVL + NBA were all locked by end of April. Pacquiao-co-branded games + integrated payment gateway also rolled forward from W18. (Source: Manila Times 5/6 · Context.ph 5/5 · GlobeNewswire 4/27)

§02Strategic Read · 5Q

Strategic Read · 5 Questions

Q1
Market structure

Two structural events this week.

· Policy ceiling is in: the May 7 PAGCOR memo capping rebates / cashback at 1.5% of turnover puts CasinoPlus (2.6%), PlayTime (2.55%), and Bet88 (5% MLB) all out of compliance. They have to rebuild promo structures within the month. The memo is explicitly framed to "level the playing field" — written to suppress oversized-rebate strategies from the heavily capitalized operators.
· Demand is cooling: DigiPlus Q1 -33% net income, -25% revenue tells you the category leader is hurting. With e-wallet delinking + fuel inflation + a rebate cap, three pressures are squeezing margin at once.

Center of gravity has fully shifted: from "acquisition via rebate" to "brand IP + scarce content + compliance moat." PlayTime's pageant crossover is the only W20 operator riding this wave.

Source: IAG 5/10 · Manila Times 5/6 · Context.ph 5/5 · GGRAsia
Q2
What players actually care about

Player concern rotated this week:

· "Is my rebate still there?" — the PAGCOR May 7 memo is circulating fast in player communities. Heavy-cashback users on CasinoPlus / PlayTime / Bet88 are asking when these brands will adjust.
· "Can I still get my money out of King and Queen?" — both sites are in week 3 of maintenance, "Loading Issue Detected" still firing.
· Withdrawal speed, KYC, customer support — the usual three are still there, just pushed below the two above.

Source: PH player community recurring topics + king.ph / queenking.ph site recon 5/11
Q3
The gap

Mother's Day 5/10 just passed — all 9 brands skipped it, as predicted in W19, zero creative anywhere. Next cultural date: payday 5/15 (T-4) and Father's Day 6/15 (T-35) — Father's Day is at zero from everyone too.

New empty quadrant: "retention narrative post-rebate-cap." The last 18 months in PH iGaming has run on cashback math. Now that PAGCOR has capped that, whoever moves the retention story from "money back" to "player identity + community belonging" first owns the next 12 months.

Another empty flag: local-IP slot content. CasinoPlus quietly dropped a wave of PH-mythology slots this week (Bakunawa 2 / Maria Makiling / Ibong Adarna / Lam-ang / Diwata-Mayari) — first operator visibly running a "local-IP" lane. The other 8 are still stacking international suppliers.

Source: site recon 5/11 + Father's Day calendar check
Q4
Pricing

Current cashback rates vs the new PAGCOR 1.5% ceiling:

· CasinoPlus 2.6% (over by +1.1pp); PlayTime 2.55% unlimited (over by +1.05pp + structure rule violation); Bet88 5% MLB weekly (over by +3.5pp); OKGames 60% Top-Up Rebate (structure needs review).
· Not affected: BingoPlus 30% JILI Super Ace cashback (in-game, not platform rebate — pending PAGCOR clarification); ArenaPlus runs on IP, not rebate; GameZone uses daily tournaments rather than rebate.

H2 pricing will converge to 1.5%, and the moat operators built on cashback math is being thinned out.

Source: each brand's /promotions page, W20 site recon 5/11 + IAG 5/10
Q5
Differentiation

W20 lanes (new + shifts):

· PlayTime crossover (NEW): iGaming + PT Idols + PT Sports all running; Miss Universe Philippines + Mister Pilipinas + MMFF + Manila Times Golf Cup + Panagbenga — repositioning the gambling brand as a full-IP entertainment group.
· DigiPlus three-brand matrix tightening: BingoPlus on celebrity + galas, ArenaPlus already locked on PBA + PVL + NBA (NBA was 4/27 PR; no W20 new move), GameZone on human-vs-human card games;
· CasinoPlus local IP (NEW): jackpot + cashback PLUS a PH-mythology slot series;
· Bet88 on supplier integration + promo stacking + Chinese UI;
· OKBet / OKGames — no visible second wave after MMD;
· King + Queen — maintenance into week 3.

Locked lanes this week: pageant / entertainment IP (PlayTime), major-league IP (DigiPlus), local-mythology slots (CasinoPlus), sustained-outage ALERT (King + Queen).

The H2 moat is heading toward "content scarcity + IP lockup." Operators built on cashback math will hurt the most.

Source: this week's 9-brand moves + PAGCOR 5/7 memo cross-check
