§02Strategic Read · 5Q

Strategic Read · 5 Questions

Q1
Market space

Post-5/15, the market splits two ways:

· Compliance side: the 1.5% turnover / 15% net-loss caps are now in force. Previously-approved-but-non-compliant promos must sunset. EGLD's EG Form No. 28 queue is set to back up.
· Marketing side: site recon shows the dominant playbook isn't "cut the number," it's "switch the label" — cashback gets repositioned as welcome / first-deposit / reload bonus, with classification subject to PAGCOR clarification. OKBet 60% Top-Up, BingoPlus 30% cashback, Bet88 100% deposit bonus all run this playbook.

If PAGCOR doesn't follow with definitions distinguishing ongoing from one-time, the 1.5% cap will be diluted by labeling. That's the watch item for the next 4–8 weeks.

Source: IAG 5/10 · GGRAsia · 9-brand site recon 5/18
Q2
Player concerns

Three things, ranked by social-channel frequency this week:

· "What happens to my money on King / Queen?" — Week 4 of outage, group silent. Withdrawal anxiety topic outranks the PAGCOR rule.
· "Is my cashback still there?" — Post-5/15, CasinoPlus 2.6% / Bet88 5% weekly users are pinging operator support. Responses vary.
· "How do I get to BingoPlus Night on 5/19?" — Activity-side topic, Vice Ganda + Kim Chiu lineup amplifying.

Source: PH player community trending + king.ph / queenking.ph site recon 5/18
Q3
Gaps

5/15 payday → 5/24 weekend → 6/12 Independence Day — three back-to-back occasions, zero brand activations this week. Philippine Father's Day (June 15) is in warm-up window — also zero moves.

"Real monthly return under the new labels" — players are catching on that 60% / 30% / 100% headline numbers are one-time on first deposit, and the actual monthly value is well below what ongoing rebates used to give. An operator that can clearly explain the real monthly return to players could capture a "compliance narrative + player trust" differentiation (execution hedge in §06 Opportunity 3).

Four weeks of King + Queen outage and zero local press coverage. If silence extends through week 5, the cost of player migration and trust recovery rises sharply.

Source: 9-brand /promotions recon 5/18 + PH press silence on King.ph keyword
Q4
Pricing

Post-5/15 gap between "what's labeled" and "what's actually claimable":

· OKBet: hero "Top-Up 60% Rebate" (actually a 60% welcome bonus, 48-hour window, 10x wagering) + 1.5% daily rebate (within PAGCOR cap)
· BingoPlus: "30% Cashback" (one-time ₱900 welcome on first ₱3K deposit) + JILI Super Ace single-game promo
· Bet88: "5% Bonus Every Week" (weekend / payday reload, classification TBD) + "100% deposit bonus" (welcome frame)
· CasinoPlus: 2.6% cashback label still up (post-5/15 classification TBD)
· PlayTime: 2.55% cashback removed, ₱7,730 single welcome offer in its place

Bottom line: the "bigger rebate number" era is over. Moats now run through IP lock-in, local-culture content, and single-SKU.

Source: each brand's /promotions page, site recon 5/18 · iGB 5/12 · RULES.md §3.2
Q5
Differentiation

W21 summary: DigiPlus three-brand stack peaks this week (BingoPlus Night) / PlayTime MUPH aftershock / CasinoPlus local IP holds / OKBet dual-track label / Bet88 multi-tier split / King + Queen player-intake window (see §04 cards for detail).

Conclusion: H2 moats are no longer about "bigger number." They're about content scarcity, IP lock-in, and transparent compliance narrative. Post-5/15, brands that ran on rebate-size differentiation are structurally squeezed; the 2026 H2 profit ranking is set to reshuffle.
