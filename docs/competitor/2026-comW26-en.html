§02Strategic Read · 5Q

Strategic Read · Five Questions (neutral)

Q1
Market space

The market is still shrinking. PAGCOR forecasts 2026 full-year GGR at ₱320-350B, down up to 19% from 2025's ₱396.14B; Q1 GGR fell 15.87% YoY to ₱87.6B, with online electronic gaming down 22.43% to ₱39.9B. Those figures are W24-W25 background; there's no fresh quarterly data this week (Q2 lands around end-July). No new product landed on the supply side this week. What actually moved is the regulatory layer: PAGCOR's 6/9 memo forces the whole market to swap responsible-gambling ads for 24/7-helpline creatives by 7/15 — pushing compliance down to the "ad scheduling" level, with a hard deadline and specific creative specs.

In a line: the market shrinks, no new supply move this week; regulation pushes compliance from "direction" to "ad scheduling," and every PH responsible-gambling slot is now on a 7/15 countdown.

Sources: AGB 6/17 · Yogonet GGR background · PNA Feb hearing
Q2
Player concerns

What players care about barely overlaps with the big industry stories — same this week. The three headlines (ad swap / DigiPlus's two lines / World Cup week two) drew little player chatter. What players keep arguing about — all backed by public data: (1) money can't get out or in (deposits stall — KYC + payment-channel limits; withdrawal delays of days to weeks are a constant complaint, with legal-advice sites flagging "withdrawal not arriving" as a red flag); (2) gambling debt and addiction (the police cybercrime unit logged ~1,891 illegal-online-gambling complaints in Q1 2026 alone; media estimate 30M+ Filipinos gamble online; the church and public-health voices call it a social crisis); (3) impersonation scams (brand names used for phishing — an old problem).

In a line: players want money in, money out, no scams, plus recovery resources. PAGCOR's helpline push lands right on the "addiction / recovery" theme players have long raised — whoever makes the swap more than box-checking, actually communicating the helpline clearly, can earn a little trust.

Sources: cybercrime Q1 complaints · gulfnews addiction crisis · withdrawal legal guide (long-running structural issues, not event-specific)
