The 6/9 memo's deadline is 7/15: every responsible-gambling placement that runs past that date must be swapped to 24/7 helpline materials beforehand, kept through 9/15, with a report due 7/16. This is the order already covered last week (W26); this week it's just the countdown. The Minimum Guaranteed Fee (MGF), live since 6/1, presses compliance and cost together. See §04 card 2.(src: asgam 6/17 · AGB MGF)

3. On-site 6/29: brands still run baseline rebates and sports tiers, none with a 6/30 payday special.
CasinoPlus's 1.5% Cashrebate runs to 7/31 (on-site read corrects the earlier "expires 6/30"); ArenaPlus's promo page mostly shows ended tiers (World Cup wrapped, NBA Finals ended 6/13); GameZone runs tiered baseline offers. World Cup (opened 6/11, runs to ~7/19) — each brand runs its own, nobody escalates. See §04 card 3 + §02 Q3.

§02Strategic Read · 5Q

Strategic Read · Five Questions (neutral)

Q1
Market size

The market is still shrinking. PAGCOR projects 2026 full-year gaming revenue of ₱32-35B, down up to 19% from 2025's ₱39.614B; Q1 online e-games fell 22.43% YoY (W24-W25 background; no new quarterly data this week, Q2 ~late July). At the same time DigiPlus keeps growing — financial media put its share of the PH online / e-games market at roughly 40-50% (figures vary, ~41% to ~48%, listed as background, not endorsed), and this week's vision launch pushes its voice higher.

One line: the market shrinks while DigiPlus alone grows; nothing launched in volume this week — what moved was DigiPlus's brand action and PAGCOR's compliance deadline.

src: insiderph share · Yogonet GGR background
Q2
Player focus

What players care about barely overlaps the big industry news; this week's three headlines drew little player chatter. Players' long-running gripes are three: money in/out friction (KYC gating + payment-channel limits, harder after GCash / Maya de-linking), gambling debt & addiction, and brand names hijacked for phishing. DigiPlus making "player protection" a concrete action (ArenaPlus wiring in Sportradar) lands on players' worry about "is it fair, is my money safe."

Read: DigiPlus making responsible gaming a visible action is more persuasive than a slogan; independents that only talk will fall behind in time — my read, not yet validated by data.

src: long-running structural issues (addiction / withdrawals / phishing) + DigiPlus vision release (player protection)
Q3
Gaps

6/30 payday window: on-site 6/29, brands run baseline rebates and sports tiers, none with a payday special. This is June's last cash-in-hand window.

Deposit education: deposits often stall (KYC + payment channels + e-wallet de-linking); not one brand runs "how to deposit / alternative rails" education. Continues W25-W26.

Spelling out responsible gaming: DigiPlus made RG a product action via Sportradar; for independents this is still slogan-level. On 7/15 everyone swaps helpline assets, but nobody makes the helpline message something players actually read and share.

src: 6/29 on-site + this week's public moves
Q4
Price structure

6/29 on-site:

Rebates still bunched at 1.5%: CasinoPlus 1.5% Cashrebate (extended to 7/31), OKBet main 1.5%, Bet88 / PlayTime 1.5% ongoing. The non-DigiPlus rebates look more and more alike. Sports tiers are winding down: NBA Finals ended 6/13, World Cup runs to ~7/19; ArenaPlus's promo page is mostly ended tiers on 6/29, CasinoPlus's World Cup Challenge ended earlier; OKBet (FIFA pool ₱15,888,888) and Bet88 (World Cup pool ₱4.9M) carried from prior. Welcome tiers: Bet88 100% first deposit ₱8,888, PlayTime ₱8,080, BingoPlus JILI 30%. DigiPlus new content: BingoPlus launched Pinoy Drop Ball Turbo (local Perya new game, 6/19).

One line: prices barely changed, independents still stuck on 1.5% rebates and World Cup pools; DigiPlus doesn't fight on price — it builds voice with a new game, the ADT golf event, and responsible-gaming action.

src: 6/29 on-site (CasinoPlus / ArenaPlus / GameZone) + OKBet / Bet88 carried from prior
Q5
Differentiation

Two layers this week: DigiPlus's three brands push the vision, the new game and NBA together; the independents (CasinoPlus / OKBet / Bet88 / PlayTime) hold rebates and World Cup tiers, no new competitive move.

Conclusion: the voice this week is DigiPlus's. It didn't lean on promos — it framed responsible gaming and social contribution as a corporate story and, in passing, raised the bar for compliance messaging.

Proposal: independents that follow DigiPlus's compliance-messaging line should cut their naming risk; but don't copy its foundation scale — the size mismatch will ring false. What's worth learning is making RG a concrete, checkable action (self-exclusion, deposit limits, third-party watch); even small beats a slogan. This is a suggestion, not something that has happened.

src: this week's public moves + DigiPlus vision multi-source + 6/29 on-site

High impact

Responsible-gambling ads must be swapped before 7/15 — run the old version past it and you're non-compliant

PAGCOR's 6/9 memo deadline of 7/15 has 16 days left. All licensees, suppliers, system administrators and venue operators must swap old responsible-gambling ads for the "24/7 National Problem Gambling Helpline" version before 7/15, keep them through 9/15, with a report due 7/16. This is the W26-reported order, now in countdown, no new directive.
DeadlineSwap before 7/15, keep through 9/15, report due 7/16
ScopeBillboards, wallscapes, digital displays and other OOH; licensees + suppliers + system administrators + venue operators
ApprovalHelpline materials exempt from Ad Standards Council; PAGCOR issues exemption certificate directly
MGF presses tooMinimum Guaranteed Fee first tranche 6/1-12/31: online e-casino below the ₱30M / ₱15M monthly GGR floors still pays ₱9M / ₱3M
Why it matters: 7/15 is a hard deadline. Any PH responsible-gambling placement still on the old version after 7/15 is non-compliant and must come down and be re-run, at more cost. Add the MGF floor — only ~25 operators clear the ₱30M monthly GGR threshold — and the squeeze is real: shrinking market, rising compliance and fixed cost together.
src: asgam 6/17 · AGB 6/17 · GGB Magazine · AGB MGF
Proposal (Client X): before entry, build one new-helpline generic layer for the full RG set (banner / card / end-card / OOH) and batch-swap before 7/15; run later assets on the new version. Bake MGF into burn — below ₱30M monthly GGR, ₱9M/mo is a net-loss floor the first-year model must price in.

§06Implications · Threats / Opportunities

Implications (Threats / Opportunities)

Entry parameters (all internal estimates)
  • RG asset swap: a new-helpline generic layer for the full RG set before 7/15 ~₱150K-₱400K one-off.
  • Cost to match the DigiPlus story: an equivalent "vision + third-party watch + foundation" narrative — governance framework + Sportradar-tier monitoring + CSR backing ~₱10M-₱30M + 6-12 months.
  • MGF net-loss floor: below ₱30M monthly GGR, ₱9M/mo is a net-loss floor; price it into the first-year model.
  • Trim entry volume: shrinking market (GGR down up to 19%) + MGF floor + DigiPlus growing — trim first-year acquisition targets 15-25%.
Threats (5)Threats
  • DigiPlus raises the compliance-messaging bar; independents are easier to name. It made RG a checkable action (Sportradar); smaller brands stuck at slogans are easier for PAGCOR to flag in a tightening cycle. Read + cost of inaction (est.): lagging compliance messaging, higher naming / placement-limit risk; remedial fixes 2-3× costlier than building ahead.
  • The 7/15 RG swap is a hard deadline; miss it and the whole set re-runs. 6/9 order, 16 days left; cross 7/15 still on the old version and you're non-compliant. Cost of inaction (est.): cross-period assets all pulled and re-run on 7/15, batch remake 2-3× costlier than swapping ahead.
  • MGF floor + shrinking market = entering against the current. 2026 GGR down up to 19%, online Q1 -22.43%; only ~25 operators clear the ₱30M monthly GGR. Cost of inaction (est.): misjudge the market as growing, first-year target 15-25% too high, MGF left out of burn, ROI down.
  • DigiPlus's content and sports assets keep pulling ahead. BingoPlus runs the ADT golf + a local new game, ArenaPlus is NBA's official PH betting partner (signed April); independents bunched in World Cup pools + 1.5% rebates. Read + cost of inaction (est.): fighting on top-tier IP, scale mismatch, acquisition diluted 30-50% by its voice.
  • Deposit friction is structural, worse after e-wallet de-linking. Deposits stall (KYC + payment channels + GCash / Maya de-linking), online Q1 -22.43%; watching only CPA, not deposit success, distorts. Cost of inaction (est.): low deposit success, CPA looks on-target but first-deposit conversion collapses, effective CAC understated 30-50%.
Opportunities (4)Opportunities
  • Making RG a checkable action — still unclaimed among independents (est. ~₱200K-₱800K one-off). DigiPlus took the lead with Sportradar, but for smaller brands this is still slogan-level. Client X can run scale-fit player protection (self-exclusion, deposit limits, transparent KYC education). Note: protection mechanisms imply compliance commitments — legal review first, no "100% safe" claim.
  • Turning helpline assets into trust assets — unclaimed (est. ~₱150K-₱400K one-off). Everyone swaps on 7/15, but swapping is an obligation, not differentiation. Client X can make the helpline message something players read and share, not just fine print. Note: helpline assets may face later PAGCOR specs / mandatory wording — wait for the official spec, don't invent; legal review first.
  • 6/30 payday / festival window un-flagged (est. ~₱500K-₱1M / 4 weeks). On-site 6/29 nobody ran a payday special. Client X can run brand-emotion comms (no promo, no family / minor imagery), "rational spending + responsible gaming." Client X can run it, turning payment friction into a trust asset. Note: alternative rails need legal review first, no "guaranteed / sure-win" wording, no steering around compliant channels.
