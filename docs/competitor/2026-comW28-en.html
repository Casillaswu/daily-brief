Three things in PH iGaming this week: PAGCOR's 7/15 ad-swap enters its final week — run the old version past it and you're non-compliant; 6/29-7/06 saw no new promo move from any competitor; No new quarterly data this week — the numbers wait for month-end; Q2 GGR lands ~late July, Senate bills stay pending.

1. PAGCOR's 7/15 responsible-gambling ad-swap enters its final week — run the old version past it and you're non-compliant.
The 6/9 memo's deadline is 7/15; this week (7/6) is in the final week. Every licensee, supplier, gaming system administrator and venue operator must swap old responsible-gambling ads for the "24/7 national problem-gambling helpline" version by 7/15, keep them through 9/15, and file a compliance report by 7/16. PAGCOR still hasn't published the asset spec (dimensions / mandatory copy). See §04 card 1.(src: asgam 6/17 · AGB 6/17)

Q1
Market size

The market itself keeps shrinking: Q1 whole-industry GGR ₱87.60B (-15.87% YoY), online e-games ₱39.90B (-22.43%); Q2 data lands ~late July.

One line: no new product, tier or competitor move this week; the only thing moving is PAGCOR's compliance deadline counting down.

src: Yogonet GGR background
Q2
Player focus

Long view: players still care about money in/out friction (KYC gating + payment-channel limits, harder after GCash / Maya de-linking), gambling debt & addiction, and brand names hijacked for phishing. The helpline swap is an operator-side duty players generally don't discuss. The Senate ban noise registers with players, but there was no new hearing this week and no new trigger.

Read: this week has little effect on player sentiment; what actually moves players is deposit friction and ban uncertainty — neither made progress this week.

src: long-running structural issues (addiction / withdrawals / phishing) + Senate ban backdrop

July calendar gap: World Cup wraps ~7/19; nobody is pre-loading the gap after the sports-tier wind-down through mid-to-late July.

Conclusion: no competitor differentiation to read this week; the second-half moat holds the W27 read — "content scarcity + IP lock-in + transparency of the compliance narrative", with rebate-only operators structurally squeezed over time.

Implications (Threats / Opportunities)

Entry parameters (all internal estimates)
  • RG asset swap: a new-helpline generic layer for the full RG set before 7/15 ~₱150K-₱400K one-off; with the spec undefined, build a conservative generic version.
  • MGF monthly fixed cost: below ₱30M monthly GGR, ₱9M/mo is gone regardless; price it into the first-year model.
  • Trim entry volume: shrinking market (GGR down up to 19%) + MGF floor + DigiPlus growing — trim first-year acquisition targets 15-25% (carried from W27).
  • Prep cost: use this quiet week to finish the 7/15 assets + August-peak plan — near-zero incremental media spend, only capacity.
Threats (5)Threats
  • The 7/15 RG swap is a firm deadline, 9 days left; miss it and every placement gets pulled and redone. 6/9 order, now T-9; cross 7/15 still on the old version and you're non-compliant. An undefined spec adds rework risk. Cost of inaction (est.): cross-period assets all pulled and re-run on 7/15, batch remake 2-3× costlier than swapping ahead; a late spec can force a second rework.
  • The market's shrinking and MGF is a fixed cost — entering right now is an uphill push. 2026 GGR down up to 19%, online Q1 -22.43%; only ~25 operators clear the ₱30M monthly GGR. Cost of inaction (est.): misjudge the market as growing, first-year target 15-25% too high, MGF left out of burn, ROI down.
  • The Senate ban stays unresolved; entry timing carries policy tail risk. The ban / regulate bills (3 ban incl. SB 47, 2 regulate) stay pending, no new hearing this week; until it settles, keep entry pace and budget commitments with an escape route. Read + cost of inaction (est.): commit all budget, and if the ban direction turns sharp, sunk cost is high and flexibility low.
  • DigiPlus's content and sports lead holds; this quiet week didn't narrow the gap. BingoPlus runs the ADT golf + a local new game, ArenaPlus is NBA's official PH betting partner (signed April); independents bunched in World Cup pools + 1.5% rebates, with no new move to close in this week. Read + cost of inaction (est.): fighting on top-tier IP, scale mismatch, acquisition diluted 30-50% by its voice.
  • Deposit friction is structural, worse after e-wallet de-linking. Deposits stall (KYC + payment channels + GCash / Maya de-linking), online Q1 -22.43%; watching only CPA, not deposit success, distorts. Cost of inaction (est.): low deposit success, CPA looks on-target but first-deposit conversion collapses, effective CAC understated 30-50%.

Note: don't force spend this week, hold budget for the peak; with the spec undefined, build a conservative generic version.

Client X can make the helpline message something players read and share, not just fine print.
