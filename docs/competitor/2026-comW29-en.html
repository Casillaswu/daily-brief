This Week

1. The OOH ad-swap deadline is 7/15, and PAGCOR still hasn't given a creative spec.
PAGCOR's 6/9 memo set the deadline at this Wednesday (7/15). Licensees, suppliers, gaming system administrators and venue operators must replace old responsible-gambling ads with materials promoting the 24/7 National Problem Gambling Helpline, keep them up through 9/15, and file a compliance report on 7/16. As of Monday, PAGCOR still hasn't given a creative spec (dimensions, mandatory wording). Two more deadlines follow: B2B accreditation on 7/31 and the MGF threshold step-up on 10/1. See §04 Card 1.(Sources: PAGCOR · asgam 6/17)

§02Strategic Read · 5Q

Strategic Read · Five Questions

Q1
Market space

All the promotional share of voice this week sits with DigiPlus. BingoPlus committed over ₱210M across two campaigns; the other 8 posted zero new offers.

The market is shrinking: Q1 industry GGR ₱8.76B (-15.87% YoY), online e-games ₱3.99B (-22.43% YoY). Q2 lands around end-July. The market is shrinking while the leader spends harder, squeezing the room for mid-tier operators.

In one line: the market is contracting and the leader is spending into it, at the same time.

Sources: Punto 7/11 · Q1 GGR background
Q2
Player concerns

The same three unresolved problems: money in and money out (KYC friction, payment-channel limits, harder deposits since the GCash / Maya delinking), gambling debt and addiction, and brand names used in phishing scams.

The Carnival's terms are worth noting: Angpao rewards require 3x turnover before withdrawal, expire in 72 hours, and Poker / PulaPuti / Slot PG don't count toward turnover.

Judgment: ₱170M is a guaranteed pool, not a guaranteed payout. What a player actually collects is far below the headline — this is where the player-side gap opens.

Source: Punto 7/11 · Carnival terms
Q3
Gaps

Differentiating the 7/15 swap: every licensee has to swap, but compliance is an obligation, not a differentiator. Nobody has turned the helpline message into something a player would read and share.

Deposit education: deposits keep failing (KYC + payment channels + e-wallet delinking) and no competitor publishes a "how to deposit / alternative rails" guide. Carried from W25-W28.

Promo-terms transparency: both BingoPlus campaigns carry three layers of restriction — turnover multiple, expiry, excluded games. No operator explains what a player actually walks away with. That's a trust gap, and the cheapest differentiator available.

Source: 8 brands with 0 new offers + Carnival terms
Q4
Offer structure

DigiPlus runs big event campaigns; everyone else runs rebates. BingoPlus is using a nine-day challenge ladder plus a baccarat tournament IP (running to 11/28) to extend engagement. CasinoPlus, OKBet, Bet88 and PlayTime are all still on the 1.5% rebate.

The 1.5% rebate is no longer a differentiator. It's the entry ticket. Four brands, same number, near-identical terms.

Sports offers are winding down: the World Cup final is 7/19, so OKBet's FIFA pool (₱15,888,888) and Bet88's World Cup pool (₱4.9M) run out with it. No successors announced. CasinoPlus's 1.5% offer expires 7/31 — the first pricing inflection to watch next week.

Sources: W27 offer reads + BingoPlus 7/1 and 7/11 releases
Q5
Differentiation

All the differentiation this week sits with DigiPlus: an owned baccarat tournament IP, a physical venue (LaVie), and capital firepower (₱5.36B buyback plus ₱20B cash). The other operators have no equivalent.

A new chokepoint appeared in the supply chain. With PhilWeb distributing Pragmatic Play exclusively, operators can only reach PP content through it. Judgment: a supplier mid-tier operators could negotiate with directly now sits behind an intermediary, and the content cost structure shifts.

Conclusion: for operators competing on rebate percentages alone, what widened this week is an asset gap, not just a budget gap.

Suggestion: don't fight BingoPlus on pool size — the weight class is wrong. Fight where it can't: show players what they actually walk away with (turnover multiple, expiry, excluded games), and turn the 7/15 swap into trust material rather than obligation material. This is a suggestion, not something that has happened.

Source: AGB 7/9 · PhilWeb × Pragmatic Play

Card 3 · PhilWeb / OKADA PLAY07/09 + 07/12
High impact

PhilWeb took exclusive Pragmatic Play licensing — operators can only reach PP content through PhilWeb

PhilWeb disclosed to the Philippine Stock Exchange on 7/9 that it holds exclusive Pragmatic Play distribution rights in the Philippines. Licensed operators can use Pragmatic Play's slots and live casino content only through PhilWeb's B2B licence. The arrangement remains subject to regulatory approval. Pragmatic Play is headquartered in Gibraltar and licensed in Great Britain, Gibraltar and Malta.

On 7/12 PhilWeb's board elected Gokongwei as a director, with a $33M personal investment confirmed. PhilWeb operates OKADA PLAY in partnership with Okada Manila.
Why it matters: this isn't a campaign, it's a change in supply-chain position. Pragmatic Play is one of the largest content suppliers in iGaming, and operators used to negotiate with it directly. Now there's a layer in between, and the party holding that layer runs a consumer product of its own (OKADA PLAY). Judgment: mid-tier operators' negotiating room, launch scheduling and exclusivity windows now sit with a party that is also a competitor, and the content cost structure shifts. After the 7/31 B2B accreditation deadline, supply-chain concentration only tightens.
💬 Social signal: ⚠️ Single source — PSE disclosure plus AGB reporting only. No other operator has responded publicly; no player-side reaction.
Sources: AGB 7/9 · PhilWeb × Pragmatic Play · AGB 7/12 · Gokongwei elected
Suggestion (for Client X): re-rank the content supplier list. If the content mix leans heavily on Pragmatic Play, price in the cost and scheduling risk of going through PhilWeb. If it isn't fixed yet, pull forward negotiations with alternatives (Evolution / JILI / PG). This is a pre-entry problem, not a post-launch one.

Card 4 · DigiPlus07/06 → 07/09
Mid impact

Three days after an investor letter, DigiPlus revived a ₱5.36B buyback

On 7/6, three investment foundations linked to Poland's Juroszek family (Betplay Capital / ZJ / MJ, together holding roughly 1.4%) sent an open letter to the DigiPlus board. It said the company is the lowest-valued listed B2C gaming operator among global peers: roughly 2.4x estimated 2026 EV/EBITDA (about a third of the peer median), with a free cash flow yield near 32% (more than six times the peer median of roughly 5%). Applying peer median multiples implies about ₱30 per share, over 150% above the then-market price. With more than ₱20B in cash (about $325M) and virtually no debt, the letter argued for putting free cash flow into repurchasing and cancelling shares rather than land-based investment.

The land-based investment it named is DigiPlus's HK$1.6B convertible note subscription in International Entertainment Corp (parent of New Coast Hotel Manila) — two tranches completed in March and June. Full conversion would give DigiPlus a 53.89% controlling stake; none has been converted yet.

On 7/9 the DigiPlus board approved reviving the share buyback at ₱5.36B (about $87M) for 12 months. The prior authorization had expired on 7/4. Shares closed at ₱11.96 on 7/8, against a 52-week range of ₱9.95-₱40.65.
Why it matters: the leader's stock is down about 70% from its high, and management is choosing to buy its own shares back rather than keep expanding. Judgment: this is capital discipline, not retreat. The ₱20B in cash is still there, and the promotional side is spending harder. What it signals is that DigiPlus doesn't think the market justifies more land-based capex — consistent with Q1 online at -22.43%. The leader is marking down its own expansion assumptions, and a new entrant's first-year target should follow.
💬 Social signal: ⚠️ Single source — financial press (AGB / Philstar). Buybacks aren't discussed player-side.
Sources: AGB 7/6 · investor letter · Philstar 7/10 · ₱5.36B buyback
Suggestion (for Client X): read this as a market thermometer. The leader at 2.4x EV/EBITDA, down 70% over 52 weeks, deciding against further land-based spend — that's a market-wide valuation discount, not a DigiPlus-specific problem. Keep the W27 first-year acquisition target cut (-15 to -25%). Don't read BingoPlus's promo spend as a market recovery.
